Overview

This project analyzes retail sales data to understand overall business performance, product mix dynamics, and sales trends over time. The analysis was conducted entirely in Microsoft Excel, focusing on structured data cleaning, PivotTable analysis, and dashboard creation to derive actionable business insights.

Dataset

Source: Retail Sales Dataset (Kaggle)

Size: 1,000 rows Ã— 9 columns

Description:
Transaction-level retail sales data including product category, customer demographics, quantity sold, pricing, and transaction dates.

Business Questions

The analysis aims to answer the following questions:

Which product categories generate the most revenue?

Is revenue driven more by sales volume or pricing across categories?

How do sales change over time, and are there observable seasonal patterns?

Tools & Techniques (Excel Only)

Data cleaning and type validation

PivotTables (Sum, aggregation, grouping by date)

Date grouping (Year and Month)

Comparative analysis (Quantity vs Revenue)

Clustered column charts

Line charts

Dashboard layout and presentation

Analysis Approach

Data Preparation

Verified data types (categorical, numeric, date)

Ensured consistent date formatting

Confirmed numeric integrity for sales-related fields

Exploratory Analysis

Revenue by product category

Quantity sold versus revenue comparison

Monthly sales trends using grouped dates

Visualization

Built a single-page Excel dashboard summarizing findings

Used appropriate chart types for each business question

Key Insights

Electronics generated the highest total sales revenue, making it the top-performing category overall.

Clothing recorded the highest sales volume but did not lead in revenue, indicating lower average unit prices.

Beauty contributed the least in both quantity and revenue.

Sales activity was concentrated in 2023, with noticeable seasonal peaksâ€”particularly around October, suggesting strong year-end performance.

Limitations

The dataset represents a limited time range, with minimal data available for 2024.

Customer-level behavioral patterns were not explored in depth.

External factors such as promotions, geography, or marketing campaigns were not included.
